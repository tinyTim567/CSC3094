Passwords

It’s important to have strong passwords for your important online accounts, such as your email, banking and social media. It’s easy to create strong, easy-to-remember passwords using the three random words rule.

Three Random Words

The longer a password is, the harder it is for cybercriminals to crack. A good way to make a strong password is to combine three random words to create a password (for example “calendargrapesmug”). This method is recommended as it allows you to create unique, easy-to-remember passwords.

What to Avoid When Creating a Password

  • Using common passwords, like 123456 or password123
  • Using significant dates, like a birthday or anniversary
  • Using the names of family members or pets
  • Swapping characters in your password, such as swapping the letter O for a zero. This doesn’t make your password significantly stronger, but it does make it harder to remember.

Aren’t passwords supposed to be very complex?

The traditional advice has been to create complex passwords; however, the National Cyber Security Centre now recommend three random words instead.

Longstanding advice around making your passwords very complex (which suggests we should create passwords full of random characters, symbols and numbers) is not helpful. This is because most of us have lots of passwords, and memorising lots of complex passwords is almost impossible.

Passwords generated from three random words is a good way to create unique passwords that are ‘long enough' and ‘strong enough’ for most purposes, but which can also be remembered much more easily. If you want to write your password down, that’s also OK, provided you keep it somewhere safe.
- National Cyber Security Centre1
