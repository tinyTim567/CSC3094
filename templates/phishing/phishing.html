Phishing

Phishing is a type of scam where an attacker attempts to deceive a user into giving away personal information, such as passwords or bank details, using scam emails, text messages or phone calls.

Phishing is one of the main methods used to commit fraud, so it's important to know how to recognise scam messages.

Reporting Fraud and Scams

You can report scam emails, calls, texts, and websites using the National Cyber Security Centre website.

If you have been a victim of fraud, you should report it to Action Fraud using their online fraud reporting tool.

How to spot a scam email

Scammers will often pretend to be someone you trust, such as your doctor or bank.

Tale-tale signs of a scam 1
1. Authority

Scammers will often pretend to be important people or organisations, such as your doctor, bank or a government department.

2. Urgency

Scammers will pressure you to respond quickly, such as within 24 hrs.

3. Emotion

Scammers may use threatening language to make you panic into responding.

4. Scarcity

Are they offering something in short supply, like concert tickets or money?

5. Current Events

Are you expecting to receive the scam message? Scammers will often exploit current news events or specific times of year, like tax reporting.
